# Articles of Incorporation — Virginia Nonstock Corporation

**For filing with the Virginia State Corporation Commission (SCC)**
Statutory basis: Virginia Nonstock Corporation Act, Va. Code § 13.1‑801 et seq.

> **How to use this:** The SCC's standard fill-in form (SCC819) does NOT contain the IRS 501(c)(3) purpose and dissolution language. To get tax-exempt status you must include it. You can either (a) file these custom Articles directly through the CIS portal as your own document, or (b) file SCC819 and attach the additional 501(c)(3) provisions. Replace every `[BRACKET]` before filing.

---

## ARTICLES OF INCORPORATION OF SAVE SNAKES, INC.

The undersigned, acting as incorporator under the Virginia Nonstock Corporation Act (Va. Code § 13.1‑801 et seq.), adopts the following Articles of Incorporation.

### Article I — Name
The name of the corporation is **Save Snakes, Inc.** (the "Corporation").

### Article II — Purpose
The Corporation is organized exclusively for **charitable and educational purposes** within the meaning of **Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code of 1986**, as amended (or the corresponding section of any future federal tax code).

Within those purposes, the specific purposes of the Corporation are to:
1. Provide free, humane, no‑kill rescue and relocation of snakes to reduce harm to people and wildlife in the Greater Richmond, Virginia community;
2. Educate the public about native snakes, snakebite safety, and peaceful human‑wildlife coexistence; and
3. Engage in any lawful activity that furthers conservation, animal welfare, and public education consistent with Section 501(c)(3).

### Article III — Nonstock / No Members (choose one before filing)
The Corporation is a **nonstock corporation** and shall **not** have authority to issue capital stock.

- [ ] The Corporation **shall have no members**. *(Recommended for a small board-governed nonprofit — simplest governance.)*
- [ ] The Corporation **shall have one or more classes of members** as set forth in the Bylaws.

> Pick one. If "no members," the board of directors holds all governance authority.

> ⚠️ **IRS note:** Virginia allows a single-director nonstock corporation, and this is fine for incorporating now. However, before filing for 501(c)(3) (Track 2), recruit at least **2 additional unrelated directors** — the IRS disfavors single-person/related boards and may flag or deny a Form 1023 from one. ### Article VI — Distribution of Assets on Dissolution
Upon the dissolution of the Corporation, after paying or making provision for all liabilities, all remaining assets shall be distributed exclusively for one or more exempt purposes within the meaning of Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code — to one or more organizations then qualifying as exempt under Section 501(c)(3) (or to a federal, state, or local government for a public purpose). Any assets not so disposed of shall be disposed of by the circuit court of the county or city in which the principal office of the Corporation is then located, exclusively for such exempt purposes.

### Article VII — Private Inurement and Activity Limitations
1. No part of the net earnings of the Corporation shall inure to the benefit of, or be distributable to, its directors, officers, or other private persons, except that the Corporation may pay reasonable compensation for services rendered and make payments in furtherance of its Section 501(c)(3) purposes.
2. No substantial part of the activities of the Corporation shall be the carrying on of propaganda or otherwise attempting to influence legislation, and the Corporation shall not participate in or intervene in (including publishing or distributing statements) any political campaign on behalf of or in opposition to any candidate for public office.
3. Notwithstanding any other provision of these Articles, the Corporation shall not carry on any activity not permitted to be carried on (a) by a corporation exempt from federal income tax under Section 501(c)(3), or (b) by a corporation, contributions to which are deductible under Section 170(c)(2) of the Internal Revenue Code.

### Article VIII — Limitation of Director/Officer Liability and Indemnification (optional but recommended)
To the full extent permitted by the Virginia Nonstock Corporation Act, no director or officer of the Corporation shall be liable to the Corporation for monetary damages, and the Corporation shall indemnify its directors and officers, except as prohibited by law.

### Filing notes
- File via the SCC Clerk's Information System: https://cis.scc.virginia.gov
- Filing fee: **~$75** (verify current amount at filing).
- After approval, download the **certificate of incorporation** and the stamped Articles — store in the records binder; you'll need them for the EIN and IRS Form 1023/1023‑EZ.
