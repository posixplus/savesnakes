## Article I — Name and Offices
**1.1 Name.** The name of the corporation is Save Snakes, Inc. (the "Corporation").
**1.2 Principal Office.** The principal office is located in [City of Richmond], Virginia. The Corporation may have other offices as the board determines.
**1.3 Registered Office/Agent.** The Corporation shall continuously maintain a registered office and registered agent in Virginia as required by law.

## Article II — Purpose
The Corporation is organized and operated exclusively for charitable and educational purposes under Section 501(c)(3) of the Internal Revenue Code, including the free, humane, no‑kill rescue and relocation of snakes and the education of the public about native snakes and human‑wildlife coexistence in the Greater Richmond, Virginia area.

## Article III — No Members
The Corporation shall have no members. All rights that would otherwise vest in members vest in the board of directors.

## Article IV — Board of Directors
**4.1 General Powers.** The affairs of the Corporation are managed by its board of directors.
**4.2 Number.** The board shall consist of no fewer than **one (1)** and no more than **[e.g., nine (9)]** directors. The Corporation intends to expand the board to at least three (3) directors, a majority of whom are not related to one another by blood, marriage, or outside business relationship, prior to applying for recognition of exemption under Section 501(c)(3).
**4.3 Term.** Each director serves a term of **[e.g., two (2)] years** and may be re‑elected. Terms may be staggered.
**4.4 Election.** Directors are elected by the affirmative vote of a majority of the directors then in office.
**4.5 Vacancies.** A vacancy may be filled by a majority of the remaining directors, even if less than a quorum.
**4.6 Removal.** A director may be removed, with or without cause, by a majority vote of the directors then in office.
**4.7 Resignation.** A director may resign by written notice to the President or Secretary.
**4.8 Compensation.** Directors serve without compensation but may be reimbursed for reasonable expenses. A director may be paid reasonable compensation only for distinct services actually rendered, subject to the Conflict of Interest Policy.

## Article V — Meetings of the Board
**5.1 Regular Meetings.** The board shall hold at least [e.g., four (4)] regular meetings per year.
**5.2 Annual Meeting.** An annual meeting shall be held to elect officers and conduct other business.
**5.3 Special Meetings.** Special meetings may be called by the President or any [two (2)] directors.
**5.4 Notice.** Notice of meetings (time, date, place/means) shall be given at least [e.g., five (5)] days in advance by email, mail, or phone. Notice may be waived in writing.
**5.5 Quorum.** A majority of directors then in office constitutes a quorum.
**5.6 Voting.** The act of a majority of directors present at a meeting with a quorum is the act of the board, except where a greater vote is required by law or these bylaws.
**5.7 Electronic Participation.** Directors may participate by conference call or video so that all can hear one another; such participation constitutes presence.
**5.8 Action Without a Meeting.** Any action may be taken without a meeting if all directors consent in writing (including email).

## Article VI — Officers
**6.1 Officers.** The officers are a President, a Secretary, and a Treasurer, and such other officers as the board may elect. One person may hold two offices, except that the same person shall not be both President and Secretary.
**6.2 Election/Term.** Officers are elected by the board and serve [one (1)]‑year terms or until a successor is elected.
**6.3 President.** Presides at meetings; provides general supervision of the Corporation's affairs.
**6.4 Secretary.** Keeps minutes and records; gives notices; maintains the corporate records binder.
**6.5 Treasurer.** Has custody of funds; keeps financial records; presents financial reports; oversees filings (Form 990, etc.).
**6.6 Removal/Vacancies.** Any officer may be removed by the board; vacancies are filled by the board.

## Article VII — Committees
The board may create committees (e.g., a Rescue Operations committee, a Finance committee). Each committee shall have [two (2)] or more directors. Committees act within authority delegated by the board.

## Article VIII — Conflict of Interest
The Corporation shall adopt and follow a Conflict of Interest Policy (see `04-conflict-of-interest-policy.md`). Each director and officer shall sign the policy's disclosure annually.

## Article IX — Contracts, Checks, Deposits, and Funds
**9.1** The board may authorize officers/agents to enter contracts on the Corporation's behalf.
**9.2** Checks or withdrawals over **$[e.g., 1,000]** require [two (2)] authorized signatures or prior board approval.
**9.3** All funds shall be deposited to the Corporation's accounts at board‑designated institutions.

## Article X — Records and Reports
The Corporation shall keep correct and complete books and records, minutes of board and committee meetings, and a current director/officer roster, and shall make required filings (IRS Form 990 series; SCC annual registration; VDACS renewal).

## Article XI — Fiscal Year
The fiscal year shall be **[e.g., January 1 – December 31]**.

## Article XII — Indemnification
To the fullest extent permitted by the Virginia Nonstock Corporation Act, the Corporation shall indemnify its directors and officers against liabilities and expenses incurred in connection with service to the Corporation.

## Article XIII — Dissolution
Upon dissolution, assets shall be distributed for exempt purposes per Section 501(c)(3) as set out in the Articles of Incorporation.

## Article XIV — Amendments
These bylaws may be amended by a [two‑thirds (2/3)] vote of the directors then in office at any meeting for which notice of the proposed amendment was given.
