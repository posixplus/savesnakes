# Conflict of Interest Policy — Save Snakes, Inc.

*Modeled on the sample policy in the IRS Form 1023 instructions (Appendix A). Adopted [DATE].*

## Article I — Purpose
This policy protects the Corporation's interest when it contemplates a transaction that might benefit the private interest of an officer or director or result in excess benefit. It supplements, and does not replace, state and federal laws governing conflicts of interest applicable to nonprofit and charitable corporations.

## Article II — Definitions
1. **Interested Person** — any director, officer, or member of a committee with board-delegated powers who has a direct or indirect financial interest.
2. **Financial Interest** — an interest, through business, investment, or family, in (a) an actual or potential transaction with the Corporation, or (b) a compensation arrangement with the Corporation or with any entity/person with which the Corporation has a transaction. A financial interest is not necessarily a conflict; a conflict exists only if the board so determines under Article III.

## Article III — Procedures
1. **Duty to Disclose.** An Interested Person must disclose the financial interest and all material facts to the directors/committee considering the transaction.
2. **Recusal.** After disclosure and discussion, the Interested Person shall leave the meeting while the transaction is discussed and voted on.
3. **Determining a Conflict.** The remaining board members decide whether a conflict of interest exists.
4. **Addressing the Conflict.**
   a. The chair may appoint a disinterested person/committee to investigate alternatives.
   b. After exercising due diligence, the board determines whether the Corporation can obtain a more advantageous transaction with reasonable effort from a person/entity that would not give rise to a conflict.
   c. If not more advantageous, the board determines by majority vote of disinterested directors whether the transaction is in the Corporation's best interest, for its own benefit, and fair and reasonable, and makes its decision accordingly.

## Article IV — Violations
If the board has reasonable cause to believe a member failed to disclose an actual or possible conflict, it shall inform the member and allow explanation. If, after hearing, the board determines a member failed to disclose, it shall take appropriate disciplinary and corrective action.

## Article V — Records
The minutes of the board and committees shall record: the names of persons who disclosed a financial interest, the nature of the interest, any determination of a conflict, and the names of persons present and votes taken.

## Article VI — Compensation
A voting member who receives compensation from the Corporation for services is precluded from voting on matters pertaining to that member's compensation. No person who receives compensation may provide information to any committee regarding their own compensation.

## Article VII — Annual Statements
Each director, officer, and committee member shall annually sign a statement affirming that they have received, read, understood, and agree to comply with this policy, and that the Corporation is charitable and must engage primarily in activities that accomplish its exempt purposes.
