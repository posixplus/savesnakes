🛡️ Safety & Resources

How to prevent snake encounters, what to do if bitten, and where to get help

🏡 How to Avoid Snakes at Your Yard & Home

Snakes are usually harmless and avoid people — but here's how to make your yard less inviting to them:

  • 🧹
    Keep your yard clean — remove brush, wood piles, and tall grass
  • 🚪
    Seal gaps — close cracks in foundations, doors, and vents
  • 🐭
    Control rodents — less food means less reason for snakes to visit
  • 💧
    Fix leaks — reduce standing water that attracts prey like frogs and insects
  • 🌱
    Trim shrubs and mulch lightly — snakes love hidden, cool spots
  • 🧼
    Keep pet food indoors — it can attract small animals that snakes hunt

💡 Good to Know

None of the commercial snake deterrents actually work! Most snakes in our area are non-venomous and helpful (they eat pests!). It's always best to give them space. Stay safe and snake-smart!

🚨 What to Do When You Are Bit

If you ever suspect a snakebite, follow these steps:

  1. Get to the hospital quickly. Call a friend or 911. Do not drive yourself.
  2. Remove constrictive clothing and jewelry from the affected area.
  3. Elevate the affected extremity. Do NOT place it below heart level.
  4. Take a picture of the snake if you can do it quickly and safely. It's helpful but not necessary. Do NOT bring the snake (dead or alive).

🚫 Do NOT Do Any of These

Tourniquets, lymphatic bandage, pressure immobilization, cut and suck, extraction device, electrical stimulation, or packing the extremity in ice.

Snakebites cause tissue injury, and improper use of ice can exacerbate that by causing thermal injury (frostbite).
